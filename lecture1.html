Developing Web Applications

Introduction to Web Development

Web development refers to the process of creating a website that may be accessed through the World Wide Web (WWW) or an internal network (intranet) (a private network). The construction of a website might include anything from the creation of a single static page consisting just of plain text to the development of complicated web apps, online companies, and social network services.

Internet definitions and history

The word "Internet" was used in 1974 by Vint Cerf and his colleagues to refer to a single worldwide TCP/IP network that was outlined in the first comprehensive specification of TCP. When all of the hosts on the ARPANET were upgraded to use TCP/IP on January 1, 1983, the first TCP/IP wide area network came into existence. Previously, those hosts had been using earlier protocols.

Static Vs Dynamic Websites

A website is considered to be static if its content does not change and visitors always see the same information regardless of the page they are on. On the other hand, a dynamic website is one that pulls material on the fly, which enables the website's content to alter depending on the person who is accessing it.

Client-Server Model

The client-server paradigm is a framework for distributed applications that divides jobs or workloads between servers, who are the suppliers of a resource or service, and clients, which are the people that seek the servers' services.

What is Internet?

The term "interconnection of computer networks" refers to what the Internet really is. The Internet is really a vast network consisting of other networks. It does this by linking millions of computers together around the globe to create a network in which any computer may speak with any other computer as long as both computers are linked to the Internet.
